 III.  REVIEW
---------------------------------------------------------------------

-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-

GRAPHICS  9/10
Graphics are sweet.  The cinemas are top notch and the word is 
detailed.  It gets a 9 because the characters are... blah.  Try 
comparing them to Final Fantasy VIII

-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-

SOUND  10/10
Nobuo Uematsu outdid himself.  From the fast paced battle music, to 
the slow beat of Aeris' theme.  Carrying in remixes of the Final 
Fantasy series, such as the Prelude, and Ending it out with one of 
the greatest pieces ever to hit a video game, One Winged-Angel.

-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-

CONTROLS 9/10
The game was easy to control, except for those pesky ladders.  
Sometimes Cloud would get stuck.  Still, for the most part, the 
controls worked fine, especially in mini games.

-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-

GAMEPLAY  10/10
If you're into RPG's, you'll like this.  This is my favorite pure 
RPG.

-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-

REPLAY  10/10
Hmmm... a 60 hour game.  Add into account trying to master all 
materias, getting every item, every weapon, and maxing out nine 
characters stats.  i've spent more time on this then most other games 
combined.

-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-

VALUE  10/10
If you have it, play it now, if not, GET IT NOW. 
It's that simple

-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-

OVERALL:  10/10
RPG's wre pretty popular before (FFVI, Chrono Trigger and a few 
others helped that), but IMO it was Final Fantasy VII that made RPG's 
famous.  Hell, it's what got me into RPGs.

-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-

HP:   1,000,000                  MORPH:         Nothing

AP:   50,000                     ITEM DROPPED:  Earth Harp

ATTACKS:  

+  Emerald Shoot
	This is Emerald Weapon's main attack.  It inflicts around 7000
 	damage to each character.  He will use this quite often.

+  Emerald Beam
	After taking enough damage, Emerald will use this attack.  It
 	does considerably less, about 5000, but some people have told me
 	it also inflicts DeSpell.  After he does this move, he will open
 	4 "eyes".

+  Eyes
	There are four of these.  Two drain about 400 MP and the other
 	two each hit for about 3000.  Each Eye has about 20000 HP.

+  Revenge Stomp
	If you destroy an eye, Emerald will stomp the entire party for
 	about 2500.  He will use this each time an eye is destroyed.

+  Aire Tam Storm
	Everyone knows about this move by now.  When you destroy all four
 	eyes, Emerald will do this.  Everyone seems to think that it will
 	always do 9999 damage.  This is far from the truth.  Aire Tam
 	Storm's damage is determined by the amount of materia that is
 	worn.  For every piece of materia, Aire Tam Storm will do 1100 
	damage.  So if you are wearing 10 or more, it will do 9999, if 
	you are wearing 3 it will do 3300, and if you are wearing no
 	materia, it will do 0000.  Square even hinted at this.  Aire Tam 
	spelled backwords is... Materia

SEQUENCE OF ATTACKS:

I've seen a lot of my friends try and just overpower Emerald without 
looking for a pattern.  Big mistake.  The thing will crush you.  Look 
at it.  He has a sequence he follows, so you can anticipate what he'll
do and prepare for it.  The sequence goes like...

1.  Emerald Shoot
	(It'll do this for awhile)
2.  Emerald Beam
3.  Open Eyes
4.  Eyes attacking and Emerald Shoot
5.  (When an eye is killed)  Revenge Stomp
6.  (When all eyes are killed)  Aire Tam Storm
7.  Repeat

So basically, it will use Emerlad Shoot.  When it uses Emerald Beam, 
watch for the eyes.  If you kill and eye, make sure you can survive 
Revenge Stomp.  When you kill all the eyes, make sure you can survive 
Aire Tam Storm.

In your materia set ups, you list that you have more than one Knights
of the Round.  How do you get more than one?

  To get more than one of a materia all you need to do is master it.
  When you master it, in the experience gain screen, it will say,
  '[materia] MASTERED' and '[materia] was born'.  This means that
  since it was mastered, it spawned another one.  The new one starts
  as if you just got it and will be at the end of the list.

-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-_-

How do you get more than one Master Magic, Master Summon, and Master 
Command?
  
  When you master all the green or red materias you can go to Bugen's 
  observatory and the Huge Materias will exchange your materias to a 
  single Master Materia.  For Command you only have to master a 
  certain seven: Deathblow, Manipulate, Mime, Morph, Sense, Steal, 
  and Throw.  You can keep doing this to your descretion.

What is the Sunken Gelnika?

  If you follow the story, Rufus mentioned Shinras gelnika plane.
  Apparently it was shot out of the sky when the weapons attacked
  cause you can find it sunk in the ocean.  It's in a small groove
  underwater just south of Junon.  What's interesting about this 
  place is that it has awesome items and you can morph all the 
  enemies here for unlimited sources.
